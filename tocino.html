Tocino

"Similar to bacon or ham, this sweet dish is best served with a morning breakfast"

Ingredients

  1. 4 and a half pounds of boneless pork shoulder
  2. 2 cups of brown suger
  3. 3 tablespoons seasoned salt
  4. 1 tablespoon garlic salt
  5. 1 tablespoon ground black pepper
  6. 1 tablespoon curing salt
  7. 5 cups of chicken broth
  8. cooking oil

Instructions

1. Cut the pork into thin slices measuring about 4-inches square; smaller pieces are fine.

2. Stir the brown sugar, seasoned salt, garlic salt, pepper, and curing salt together in a bowl; sprinkle each of the slices of pork with the mixture. Place the pork slices flat in a large container with a cover. Let them sit in the refrigerator 3 to 4 days before using.

3. Combine the desired amount of the pork with enough water to cover in a large non-stick skillet and bring to a boil for about 5 minutes. Drain and discard the water. Dry the pan completely.

4. Heat the oil in the skillet over medium heat; fry the pork slices in the hot oil until lightly browned, 1 to 2 minutes per side.
